Dr. Muhammad Shafeeque

Post-Doctoral Fellow

Dr. Muhammad Shafeeque joined as Post-Doctoral Fellow in September 2023 at the Applied Center for Environment & Marine Studies, Research Institute, KFUPM, KSA. His current research primarily focuses on “Biophysical coupling and Chlorophyll-a variability in the Arabian Gulf and Red Sea: An integrated approach using remote sensing and modeling.” He actively collaborates with multidisciplinary research groups to enhance project outcomes.

Dr. Shafeeque is a seasoned Physical Oceanographer with over ten years of experience, including his doctoral research at Cochin University of Science and Technology (CUSAT), Kochi, India. He holds a Master’s degree in Oceanography and a Bachelor’s degree in Physics. Throughout his career, he has worked at prestigious institutions such as the Central Marine Fisheries Research Institute (CMFRI), Centre for Marine Living Resources and Ecology (CMLRE), and the Indian Institute of Technology (IIT), Chennai. He has also collaborated with renowned international institutions, including the Plymouth Marine Laboratory, UK, and the Nansen Environmental and Remote Sensing Center, Norway.

Dr. Shafeeque’s expertise spans data processing, software applications, and numerical modeling. His research interests include physical processes and biological implications, phytoplankton phenology, long-term trends, climate change, bio-optical properties, and extreme weather events. Recently, he has gained proficiency in using the MIKE model for oil spill and sediment transport studies.

He is proficient in various programming languages and data processing tools such as FORTRAN, FERRET, R, Python, ODV, QGIS, and SeaDAS. His work is supported by advanced knowledge of Linux and Windows operating systems. Additionally, he has participated in coastal and deep-sea cruises, gaining hands-on experience in oceanographic observations and sample collection. Dr. Shafeeque’s scientific writing skills are demonstrated through peer-reviewed publications, which have garnered significant recognition through citations. He has received best paper presentation awards at national and international conferences.

Dr. Shafeeque is a recipient of the POGO Fellowship awarded by the Nippon Foundation. He has also received numerous travel grants from organizations such as the International Ocean Colour Coordinating Group (IOCCG), European Union, European Space Agency (ESA), and the Nansen Environmental and Remote Sensing Center, Norway, enabling him to attend international conferences, symposiums, and training programs.
